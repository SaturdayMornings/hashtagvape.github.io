Welcome!

What is the difference between people who talk about cigarettes, vapes, and Ecigs on Twitter? We've analyzed about 75,000 tweets from users who use the hashtags #Ecigs, #cigarettes, and #vape to find out. The journey began when we:

  1. Crawled twitter for Tweets containing the termss #Ecigs, #cigarettes, and #vape
  2. Took every user handle that was present in the collected tweets, and collected each and every single one of their posts to understand their habits and popularity.
  3. Analyzed these users and copared them on the following attributes:
    • Number of followers
    • Statuses count
    • Retweet count
What did we approach this experiment expecting to find?

Topic Modeling

We used LDA to generate topics based on user bios across our dataset. We identified four main topics that showed up in the bios across the dataset. These were: sale of cigarettes, health and public opinion, personal, and vape products. There were also various topics identified by LDA that we could not classify, and therefore were classified as other. Notably, there were higher percentages of users tweeting #cigarette that tweeted about health and public opinion than with #vape and #ecig users. There were no tweets classified as personal for #cigarette users, but the personal topic came up with both #vape and #ecig users. When we created word clouds out of both the bios and the tweets from our data set, both vape and ecig users were seen to tweet with significant frequency about specifically vape-related topics.
